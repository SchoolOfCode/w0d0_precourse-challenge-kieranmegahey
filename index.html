Valentino Rossi

The greatest motorcycle racer of all time.

Here's a timeline of Valentino Rossi's Career:

  • 1979 - Born in Urbino, Marche, Italy
  • 1984 - Parents buy Valentino a racing kart and upgrade 60cc motor to 100cc national kart motor.
  • 1990 - Rossi wins regional kart championship and takes up minimoto.
  • 1993 - First Motorcycle Race - Rossi rode a Cagiva Mito 125cc motorcycle, which he damaged in a first-corner crash no more than a hundred metres from the pit lane.
  • 1997 - Rossi signs for the aprilia official team and wins his first world title with his RS125 after winning 11 races. And there begins the leadership of the king of motorcycling.
  • 1999 - Rossi wins the 250cc world Championship, collecting five pole positions and nine wins.
  • 2001 - Rossi wins his first 500cc world championship title
  • 2002 - The premier class is renamed "Motogp" - Rossi wins the World championship on a 4 stroke 990cc Honda
  • 2003 - Rossi wins the World championship with Honda and announces his move to Yamaha for the next season.
  • 2004 - Rossi joins Yamaha and wins the MotoGP World Championship with the YRZ-M1.
  • 2005 - Rossi's streak continues. He wins the MotoGP World Championship again for Yamaha.
  • 2006 - Rossi's streak comes to an end. He loses to the title by 5 points to Nicky Haden after a fall in the final race in Valencia.
  • 2008 - Critics started to doubt if he was capable of ever winning another title, despite these claims Rossi takes his eighth world title, ahead of Casey Stoner.
  • 2009 - Rossi wins his ninth and final world title for Yamaha.
  • 2021 - Rossi announces his retirement from Motogp and leaves the sport with 9 world titles

"Riding a race bike is an art - a thing that you do because you feel something inside."
